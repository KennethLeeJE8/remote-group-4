Steganography (n.): "The art or practice of concealing a message, image, or file within another message, image, or file."

To accomplish our promise to artists and other visual creators, Trace employs steganography to subtly mark up images intended for a closed audience. In the case that the image is ever released elsewhere without the creator's authorization and discovered, the leaked image can be uploaded back into Trace to identify who released the image without permission.

Our Method:

When you decide to share a private image with somebody else through Trace, we add a "fingerprint" to the image before it is sent to the recipient's computer. Our reason to use steganography as opposed to watermark generation is that the visible effects of stegaonography are almost imperceptible to the eye. When our software modifies your image before serving it, many individual pixels of the image are slightly changed in order to have the fingerprint "blend in" with the data.

Once an image is reuploaded for evaluation Trace can interpret the fingerprint and determine who downloaded the image. What you do with the information is up to you alone, but we do offer you the ability to restrict the offender's access to your images in the future.
